Security Engineering

Purposes

Security engineering is the practice of designing and implementing security measures to protect systems, networks, and applications from unauthorized access, modification, or destruction.

The purpose of security engineering is to ensure that security is integrated into the design and development of systems, rather than being an afterthought or add-on. By incorporating security engineering principles, organizations can create more secure systems that are resilient to cyber attacks.

Principles

These are principles we can use in this field:

  1. Defence in Depth:

    Implementing multiple layers of security controls to provide redundancy and minimize the impact of a single point of failure.

  2. Least Privilege:

    Granting users and systems the minimum level of access necessary to perform their functions, to minimize the risk of privilege escalation.

  3. Seperation of Duties:

    Separating critical functions and ensuring that no one person or system has complete control over them, to prevent unauthorized access or misuse.

  4. Fail-safe Defaults:

    Designing systems with secure defaults, so that even if configuration errors or vulnerabilities are present, the system remains secure.

Techniques:

These are the techniques used in this field:

  1. Cryptography:

    Using cryptographic algorithms and protocols to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data.

  2. Access Control:

    Implementing access controls to limit access to sensitive information and resources, and ensure that access is granted based on the principle of least privilege.

  3. Vulnerability Mangement:

    Identifying and remediating vulnerabilities in systems, networks, and applications to reduce the risk of exploitation.

  4. Secure Coding Practices:

    Writing secure code that is resistant to common vulnerabilities such as buffer overflows, injection attacks, and cross-site scripting.
