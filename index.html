What is Cyber Security

Cybersecurity is the protection of internet-connected systems such as hardware, software and data from cyberthreats. The practice is used by individuals and enterprises to protect against unauthorized access to data centers and other computerized systems. Cybersecurity is also instrumental in preventing attacks that aim to disable or disrupt a system's or device's operations. The technique of protecting internet-connected systems such as computers, servers, mobile devices, electronic systems, networks, and data from malicious attacks is known as cybersecurity. We can divide cybersecurity into two parts one is cyber, and the other is security. Cyber refers to the technology that includes systems, networks, programs, and data. And security is concerned with the protection of systems, networks, applications, and information.

Scope of Cyber Security

As technology continues to advance, the scope of cyber security becomes increasingly vital in protecting digital assets, including hardware, software, and data, from cyber attacks, theft, or damage. With the growth of the internet, cloud computing, and the Internet of Things, the need for cybersecurity has expanded beyond traditional industries to include all sectors of society. As a result, cybersecurity professionals are in high demand, making it a promising field with many job opportunities.
